Birth Registration Song And Poem Composition 2021

Guidelines

Everyday of the year, a child is born; a precious, special and beautiful gift, one of a kind. In Africa, we celebrate the birth of a child with music, dance and poetry.

NIRA recognizes the birth of each child through the child’s registration and assignment of a unique identification number upon registration.

In the Month of December 2021, NIRA will invite children between 5 to 16 years, to enter a national song and poem competition of not longer than 2 minutes on birth notification, registration or certification. The song should capture key aspects of notification, registration and certification of births and indicated in how to register birth and death guide. Click here to download.

Guidelines for entries

  1. Compositions may be done individually or in groups.
  2. All songs and poems submitted must be original and never performed before
  3. The song or poem must be NOT be more than 2 minutes long
  4. The composer(s) of the song or poem must be willing to surrender the Intellectual Property Rights (IPR) for the composition to NIRA.
  5. The parent/s or persons with parental responsibility of the child of all the entrants will be required to sign deed of assignment of IP rights
  6. 1st, 2nd and 3rd runner ups in the following age categories will be recognized and a small token of appreciation provided towards the child’s school fees
    • 5 -8 years
    • 9-12 years
    • 13-16 years
  7. The best selected song(s) or poem(s) will subsequently be used to promote birth notification, registration and certification
  8. Songs and poems can be composed in any of the languages recognized in Uganda
  9. Entrants for the competition should clearly indicate if they are registering as individuals or groups

You can track this event on our Website www.nira.go.ug and social media platforms under the hashtag #birthsong
